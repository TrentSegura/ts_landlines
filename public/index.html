Landlines is an initiative developed by M12 Studio in order to create contemporary art installations and new cultural work throughout Colorado's rural San Luis Valley. In collaboration with artists, researchers and community partners, we have allowed for projects to be responsive and interpretive of the complexity embedded in the unique cultural ecosystem of the region. Visiting artists and regional partners have worked on projects between 2018-2021 in their areas of expertise and are also challenged to think collectively about cultural preservation, environmental justice and rural futures. M12’s partners in the San Luis Valley highlight a range from adobe architecture to potato farming, water rights to embroidery, Hispano folk music to new age spiritual movements, and off-road and DIY homestead enthusiasts.

Through Landlines, M12 Studio provides support and space for artists to be involved in this work and our community for the Landlines Initiative is defined geographically and relationally. Each of the projects produced are considerate of such a diverse region. Landlines has resulted in numerous low-impact art installations throughout the region. In the time of COVID we are not able to celebrate this work through our usual potluck dinners, block parties and workshops, so we offer this map and its contents as a celebration of the San Luis Valley and the creative energy which it contains.
